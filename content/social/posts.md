The average appointment is now 3 weeks away.
This isn't a pandemic hangover. The trend started in 2017.

## HOUSING

In 1997, the average house cost around 3.5× the average annual salary.

Same country. Different arithmetic.

whatisactuallyhappening.uk/housing

**12.**
The age of the average first-time buyer in England has risen from 29 in the late 1990s to around 34 today.
That's not laziness or avocado toast. That's 5 years of compound interest on a deposit that keeps moving.

whatisactuallyhappening.uk/housing

---

**13.**
Surprising: house prices in parts of the North East are still below 4× local earnings.
Affordability is extremely uneven. The national average conceals two different countries.

whatisactuallyhappening.uk/housing

**14.**
London's house price to earnings ratio peaked above 12× in 2016 and has actually come down slightly since.

**15.**
Private rent as a share of median income has risen in every English region over the last decade.
There is no region where it got more affordable.

The social housing waiting list is longer than it's been at any point since records began.

Broken down by company, the variation is enormous.

Not because police don't investigate. Because of what happens after investigation.
